How did you get a job at Google or Facebook?

Recently accepted a role at Google.
My interview process:
1. Employee referral (CV referred by current Googler)
2. Phone Screen with recruiter
3. Phone interview with current employee
4. Flown to office for onsite, had 4 interviews in total (45 mins each)
5. Interview feedback and CV sent for review by Hiring Committee
6. Final reviews by SVP and senior leadership
7. Recruiter called to extend offer… which I accepted

If you want a role, you should really get a current employee to refer you. With so many talented people applying for a limited pool of open roles it really helps your chances to speak with someone and not be lost in the system.

What life lessons has your work taught you?

Over and over again, I see people in corner offices who, on paper, are of pretty mediocre intelligence.
If they were pitted against you on an academic test, most of you smart Quorans would best them fairly easily.

It means nothing.

Your SAT score.
Your board rankings.
Your perfect grades.
They evaporate. Gone. They were the price of entry.
Being smart certainly matters. It helps.
But the longer you spend in the business world, the less you’ll see success manifesting itself as a product of smarts.
